## Highest-Risk Findings

### P0 - Operational upload and mutation routes still expose weak API boundaries

Evidence:

- `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/dispatch.route.ts:75` defines dispatch routes with `publicProcedure`.
- Dispatch status/workflow mutations include `deletePackingItem`, `cancelDispatch`, `startDispatch`, `submitDispatch`, delivery updates, driver/due-date/status updates, `sendSaleForPickup`, `signPackingSlip`, duplicate-resolution, `deleteDispatch`, and `uploadDispatchDocument` on public procedure boundaries.
- `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/dispatch.route.ts:277` checks `ctx.userId` inside `createDispatch`, but nearby public mutations do not show the same boundary-level guard in the router.
- `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/dispatch.route.ts:424` exposes `uploadDispatchDocument` as a public mutation accepting caller-supplied file content.
- `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/user.route.ts:74` exposes `uploadDocumentAsset` as a public mutation that writes base64 content to employee document storage.

Impact:

Dispatch proof, packing state, delivery status, employee documents, and blob storage are accountability-critical. Public procedure boundaries make it harder to prove who changed what, and increase risk of unauthenticated writes, storage abuse, or operational status tampering unless every downstream function independently guards perfectly.

Inference:

Some downstream query functions may still check `ctx.userId` or permissions. The risk is boundary inconsistency: the router contract itself does not make authentication/authorization obvious or uniform for high-value operational writes.

### P1 - Dealer quote requests remain mutable after submission

Evidence:

- `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/dealer-portal.route.ts:380` exposes `dealerPortal.saveQuote`.
- `apps/api/src/db/queries/dealer-portal-sales-form.ts:355` loads an existing quote by id, dealer owner, non-deleted state, and `type: "quote"` only.
- The save path updates the existing quote at `apps/api/src/db/queries/dealer-portal-sales-form.ts:417`, deletes/recreates all quote line items at `apps/api/src/db/queries/dealer-portal-sales-form.ts:438`, and rewrites dealer totals at `apps/api/src/db/queries/dealer-portal-sales-form.ts:467`.
- No request-status guard is visible in that save path to block edits after `requestQuoteOrder`, approval, or rejection.

Impact:

A dealer can potentially alter door dimensions, HPT/moulding/service rows, delivery mode, pricing, or customer-facing totals after requesting conversion. Sales reps may approve a moving target, weakening delivery-cost review, payment-link trust, customer expectations, and production readiness.
