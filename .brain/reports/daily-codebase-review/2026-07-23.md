## Highest-Risk Findings

- P1 - Operational mutations still use public route boundaries. Evidence: `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/dispatch.route.ts` exposes `deletePackingItem`, `cancelDispatch`, `startDispatch`, `submitDispatch`, `signPackingSlip`, `deleteDispatch`, `uploadDispatchDocument`, `bulkAssignDriver`, `bulkCancel`, and `restore` as `publicProcedure`; `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/inventories.route.ts` exposes inventory delete/import/reset/save/product-kind/status/cost mutations as `publicProcedure`; `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/jobs.route.ts` exposes job restore/reassign/payment cancellation and job creation through `publicProcedure`. Some functions check `ctx.userId` inline, but the boundary is inconsistent and not permission-shaped for mixed-skill operational accountability.
- P1 - Full typecheck is blocked by storefront contract drift. Evidence: `bun run typecheck` fails in `@gnd/storefront` with UI, product search, Zod resolver, missing dependency, and tRPC transformer diagnostics. Storefront cutover is active in Brain, so this blocks confidence in customer-facing launch readiness even though today's explicit review scope focused on www/dealership/mobile/API/shared packages.
- P1 - Dealer quote handoff can still be undermined by edit/save after request state. Evidence: `apps/dealership/src/components/dealer-portal/dealer-sales-documents.tsx` and `apps/dealership/src/components/tables/quotes/columns.tsx` still render `Edit` for quotes whose request button is disabled due to `pending`, `approved`, or `rejected`; `packages/db/src/queries/dealers.ts` updates an existing dealer quote by `id` and `dealerAuthId` without checking latest `DealerSalesRequest` status before rewriting quote data and line items. This can confuse office approval, customer-visible totals, and dealer accountability.
- P2 - Mobile dispatch completion proof is client-orchestrated instead of atomic/resumable. Evidence: `apps/expo-app/src/features/dispatch/components/dispatch-detail-screen/index.tsx` uploads attachments and signature first, then calls `onSubmitDispatch`; `apps/expo-app/src/features/dispatch/api/use-dispatch-actions.ts` sends only completed upload pathnames into the update-sales-control task. A network interruption between upload and completion can leave proof files without a completed dispatch, or a completed dispatch without a fully auditable retry story.

## Worker Usability Review

The current product has useful operator surfaces, but the accountability model is still too implicit for mixed-skill teams. Workers can see disabled buttons and status labels, yet several server routes still trust broad public procedures or route-local checks. That makes training harder: staff can learn the visible workflow, but forged/stale paths may still mutate dispatch, inventory, jobs, or payments.

The mobile dispatch completion screen has a trainable shape: recipient, completion type, note, signature, and optional photos. The weak part is reliability. Field staff need a clear "saved proof / completed dispatch / retry pending" state, especially around delivery photos and customer signature.

## Customer And Dealer Experience Review

The dealer approval flow is clearer than prior runs: direct conversion is forbidden and the dealer UI shows request state. The remaining edit path is the trust gap. Once a dealer has requested conversion, the quote should become a locked revision or a deliberate "revise request" workflow, not a normal edit.

Sales document sharing no longer appears to hard-code a recipient in the live `SalesMenu.Share` path. It still generates a 7-day tokenized URL and uses generic share copy rather than the tracked WhatsApp/SMS/short-link plan with recipient validation, short links, channel result accounting, and missing-phone feedback.

## Door-Manufacturing Workflow Gaps

- Production readiness still depends on open inventory proof gates. Door manufacturing needs stock, inbound, allocation, production, and dispatch readiness to agree before workers treat an order as buildable.
- Quote/order approval still lacks a visible locked revision model for dealership handoff. In door workflows, a late change to swing, size, profile, delivery, moulding, or HPT rows can invalidate pricing, production readiness, and customer expectations.
- The codebase has many door/HPT/moulding/shelf parity fixes in progress, but there is not yet a simple pre-production checklist that summarizes dimension, hinge/swing, profile, supplier, stock, inbound, pickup/delivery, and print/PDF readiness in one manager-friendly gate.
