## Worker Usability Review

- Sales reps: Inventory tab language is much more trainable than raw legacy statuses, especially read-only fulfilled states. The remaining weakness is action clarity: Create inbound and Mark all available need clear eligibility reasons and a safe next step.
- Production staff: Inventory readiness gates are heavily planned and partially implemented, but Brain still records missing Phase 6 operator proof. Do not train production to rely exclusively on the new inventory state until reconciliation and proof gates close.
- Warehouse/inventory staff: Inbound and allocation correctness work is strong, but the sales overview UI still needs remaining-stock CTAs and collapsible inbounds so users can scan what is ordered, received, and still needed.
- Installers/drivers: Pickup packing and dispatch mobile work are active. The next usability opportunity is proof-oriented mobile closure: barcode/photo/signature style evidence for pick, pack, delivery, and customer handoff.
- Office/admin staff: Error boundaries and empty/loading states are broadly present across route surfaces. The risk is not blank screens; it is too many overlapping legacy/new states during cutover.
