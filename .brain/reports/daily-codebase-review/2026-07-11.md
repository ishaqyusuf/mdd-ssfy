Some downstream functions check `ctx.userId`, but the route layer is inconsistent. `prepareNonProduceablePacking` even falls back to author id `1` when no user is present.
  - Evidence: `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/hrm.route.ts:154` exposes `updateEmployeeRole` as `publicProcedure` and performs role deletion/recreation plus session deletion inline without an adjacent auth guard. `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/hrm.route.ts:120` similarly exposes employee profile updates publicly.
  - Evidence: `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/inventories.route.ts:600` through `719` includes public destructive/importing inventory mutations such as delete inventory/category/subcomponent, Dyke upserts, full import trigger, and Dyke import update. `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/settings.route.ts:71` exposes `updateSetting` publicly.

## Worker Usability Review

- Sales and office staff need fewer hidden states around quote/request/order transitions. Once a dealer asks for approval, the quote should clearly become read-only or move into a "revision requested" lane with visible ownership.
- Warehouse and dispatch staff benefit from the new mobile packing/dispatch proof shape, but completion should not depend on a fragile multi-step upload-then-submit chain without retry evidence. A mixed-skill worker needs one clear "not submitted yet" state and one reliable retry action.
- Production and inventory staff need explicit "inventory-backed coverage" labels until reconciliation is clean. Otherwise legacy-only orders and inventory-backed orders can appear equally authoritative in dashboards.
- Admin staff need a route-level permission model they can reason about. Public tRPC routes with downstream helper checks are not trainable; admin-visible permissions should map to protected procedures and server-side tests.

## Customer And Dealer Experience Review

- Dealer request status labels are present and duplicate request actions are disabled, which is good. The missing piece is hard quote immutability after request. A dealer should see whether a quote is editable, pending staff review, approved as an order, rejected, or requires a revision.
- Payment-link and PDF work is actively improving, but broad document typecheck failure remains a release-readiness issue because customer trust depends on reliable print/download/email flows.
- Customer/dealer communication opportunities remain strong: approval emails, payment links, quote delivery over WhatsApp/SMS, pickup/delivery status notifications, and "revision requested" dealer messages should converge into one consistent document communication ledger.

## Door-Manufacturing Workflow Gaps

- Requested dealer quotes do not have a manufacturing-ready freeze. Door dimensions, swing/handing, profile, HPT rows, moulding, shelf items, delivery mode, and customer profile pricing should not silently change after a sales rep reviews the quote.
- Inventory cutover still lacks clean evidence across all sales, so production readiness and ship-available workflows should keep fallback labels and manager review cues.
- Dispatch completion captures signature/photos, but there is no visible barcode/QR scan step for verifying the exact order, package, or door bundle before pickup/delivery completion.
- The current public-route pattern makes it hard to guarantee that stock imports, inventory deletes, dispatch changes, and HRM role edits always have the right actor attached for shop-floor accountability.
