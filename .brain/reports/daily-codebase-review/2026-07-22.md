### P1 - Sales document Share can still send tokenized PDFs to a fixed phone number

Evidence: `apps/www/src/components/sales-menu.tsx:571` creates a seven-day sales PDF token, builds a download URL at `apps/www/src/components/sales-menu.tsx:577`, and calls `share` with `recipient: "+234 8186877306"` at `apps/www/src/components/sales-menu.tsx:582`.

### P2 - Dealer delivery-cost approval still depends on `window.prompt`

Evidence: `apps/www/src/components/dealer-request-review-banner.tsx:20` asks for reviewed delivery cost with `window.prompt`, parses the number at `apps/www/src/components/dealer-request-review-banner.tsx:27`, and sends it directly into `approveDealerSalesRequest` from `apps/www/src/components/dealer-request-review-banner.tsx:124`.

Why it matters: the dealership approval path is now QA-proven, but a browser prompt is poor for mixed-skill office staff. It gives no structured reason, no visible rate context, weak validation, and no ergonomic correction path before converting a dealer quote into an order.

### P2 - Mobile dispatch completion proof remains client-orchestrated

Evidence: `apps/expo-app/src/features/dispatch/components/dispatch-detail-screen/index.tsx:1185` may pack pickup work first, `apps/expo-app/src/features/dispatch/components/dispatch-detail-screen/index.tsx:1193` uploads each attachment, `apps/expo-app/src/features/dispatch/components/dispatch-detail-screen/index.tsx:1210` uploads the signature, and only then `apps/expo-app/src/features/dispatch/components/dispatch-detail-screen/index.tsx:1221` submits dispatch completion.

Why it matters: drivers and warehouse workers often work with weak connectivity. This sequencing can leave uploaded proof without completion, or a completion retry story without a clear proof bundle. The user experience needs a resumable, visible outbox or one server-owned completion bundle.

## Worker Usability Review

- Sales reps still have too many legacy/current document and share paths. A trainable workflow should make the safe action obvious: Email, WhatsApp, SMS, Print, Download, each with recipient preview and audit result.
- Office dealership approval has the right business model now, but delivery review should be a structured mini-form with selected delivery mode, suggested/default cost, manual override reason, and approver confirmation.
- Warehouse/driver mobile dispatch flows are feature-rich, but the final proof step should behave like a checklist with retryable proof status. Workers should not need to understand whether a photo uploaded but the dispatch completion mutation failed.
- Inventory staff should continue seeing disabled/read-only states for terminal orders, but broad cutover claims should stay blocked until Phase 8 and operator proof are clean.

## Customer And Dealer Experience Review

- Dealer quote-to-order is much stronger after the 2026-07-19 QA pass, especially dual customer/internal financial surfaces and customer-payment separation from GND payable.
- The hard-coded Share action can still harm customer trust by sending customer documents to the wrong number.
- Dealers would benefit from clearer delivery approval output: "reviewed delivery cost", who approved it, and why the amount changed should be visible in the approval history and invoice context.
- Customer-facing payment/document links should move toward the planned short-link/channel model so the message body is readable and traceable.

## Door-Manufacturing Workflow Gaps

- Production readiness still depends on inventory correctness and backfill cleanup; do not treat the fulfillment system as release-clean until the stopped repair loop is explicitly resumed or product scope is narrowed and documented.
- Door quote quality would improve with structured checks for dimensions, hinge/swing, profile/door supplier, HPT door options, and delivery mode before a quote becomes an order. Some of this is covered by new sales form parity work, but the approval surface should summarize exceptions in office language.
- Dispatch proof needs to connect photos/signature, packed items, and delivery/pickup completion as one auditable manufacturing handoff.
