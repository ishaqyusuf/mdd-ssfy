## Highest-Risk Findings

### P1 - Operational API route boundaries are still inconsistent across high-value workflows

Evidence:

- `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/dispatch.route.ts` imports only `publicProcedure` and exposes operational mutations such as `deletePackingItem`, `cancelDispatch`, `startDispatch`, `submitDispatch`, `updateDispatchDriver`, `updateDispatchStatus`, `createDispatch`, `deleteDispatch`, `uploadDispatchDocument`, `bulkAssignDriver`, `bulkCancel`, and `restore`.
- `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/inventories.route.ts` still shows `publicProcedure` on inventory writes/imports such as `deleteInventories`, `deleteInventoryCategory`, `upsertShelfProducts`, `upsertComponents`, `runFullImport`, `inventoryUpdateFromDyke`, `dykeUpdateFromInventory`, `resetInventorySystem`, `saveInventoryCategory`, `saveInventory`, `updateVariantCost`, and variant/category mutation routes.
- `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/hrm.route.ts`, `settings.route.ts`, `notes.route.ts`, `user.route.ts`, and `sales.route.ts` also expose several mutation-capable surfaces through `publicProcedure`.

Why this matters for a door manufacturer:

Dispatch, packing, inventory imports, employee access, sales payments, and notification settings are accountability-critical. Mixed-skill teams need the system to make the safe path obvious and traceable; weak route boundaries make it easier for accidental actions, stale clients, or incorrectly scoped frontends to mutate operational state without the expected role/employee context.

Tracking label: Already tracked through Brain API/permission docs and repeated daily review findings, but still unresolved in code.

### P1 - Dealer approval is QA-complete, but the legacy direct conversion mutation still exists beside it

Evidence:

- `.brain/features/dealership-quote-to-order-approval.md` says the desired product change is to replace dealer-owned direct conversion with a sales-rep approval request workflow, and the workflow is production-code complete after 2026-07-19 QA.
- `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/dealer-portal.route.ts` still exposes `convertQuoteToOrder: dealerProtectedProcedure`, calling `convertDealerPortalQuoteToOrder(ctx.db, ctx.dealer.id, input.id)`.
- The same router also exposes `requestQuoteOrder`, so both the new approval path and older direct conversion path remain present.

Why this matters:

Dealership quote-to-order approval is now a control point for sales ownership, delivery-cost review, dealer/customer invoice separation, and GND-vs-dealer receivables. Keeping a direct conversion mutation increases the chance that a dealer-facing or stale client path bypasses the manual approval workflow that the business just validated.

Tracking label: Plan exists / Already tracked in `.brain/features/dealership-quote-to-order-approval.md`.

### P1 - Mobile dispatch completion proof is split across document upload and completion mutation

Evidence:

- `apps/expo-app/src/features/dispatch/components/dispatch-detail-screen/index.tsx` uploads attachments and signature first through `documents.uploadBase64` / `documents.uploadText`, then calls `actions.onSubmitDispatch`.
- `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/dispatch.route.ts` exposes `uploadDispatchDocument` as `publicProcedure`.
- `apps/api/src/db/queries/dispatch.ts` `signPackingSlip` has an auth guard and then packs/submits dispatch, sends completion notification, expires packing/order snapshots, and queues warmups.

Inference:

Because uploads and completion are client-orchestrated, a network loss after upload but before submit can leave proof assets not tied to a completed dispatch. A failure after completion but before UI refresh can also confuse drivers about whether the trip is done. This is especially risky for installers/drivers with variable connectivity and limited time at a job site.

Tracking label: Already tracked through mobile dispatch/update and shared document platform migration work, but the current source still shows the gap.

## Worker Usability Review

Sales reps:

- Dealer request queues are now much stronger after the 2026-07-19 QA pass, but delivery-cost approval through `window.prompt` is too fragile for daily sales operations. A small review form with delivery mode, ZIP/address snapshot, current cost, required cost field, and approve/reject actions would be more trainable.
- Sales document Share remains unsafe until it resolves recipients from the active customer/dealer context and respects channel preferences.

Production/warehouse:

- Inventory-backed readiness is improving, but Brain still records non-clean reconciliation and paused repairs. Production and warehouse staff should see inventory-backed statuses as "review needed" until Phase 8 and browser/operator gates are clean.
- The public route boundary pattern around inventory/dispatch writes weakens accountability. In practice, supervisors need to know who changed stock, who assigned a driver, who completed a dispatch, and why.

Drivers/installers:

- Mobile dispatch completion has the right field proof ingredients: attachments, signature, received-by, notes, and completion. The sequence is still brittle because proof upload and completion are separate client steps.
- The mobile UI should eventually make the final state unambiguous after flaky network retries: "proof saved", "dispatch completed", or "needs resubmit".

Office/admin:

- Admin settings, employee, notification, and document review routes need consistent protected boundaries and role gates. Mixed-skill office users should not have to rely on app navigation alone as the security model.

## Customer And Dealer Experience Review

- Dealer quote-to-order approval is now well-covered by Brain and QA evidence, including separate customer/internal PDF values, dealer-owned customer payments, and dashboard earnings.
- The remaining direct conversion mutation is the main customer/dealer trust risk because it can bypass sales rep ownership and reviewed delivery cost.

- Dealership recruitment/customer privacy is promising but should remain inactive until schema apply, restart, and final public-token/customer-sharing proof are complete.

## Door-Manufacturing Workflow Gaps

- Door size, HPT, moulding, shelf item, and custom component parity remain active workstreams. These are important because quote errors become production errors: wrong dimension, swing/hinge/profile, supplier pricing, or missing moulding can propagate to purchasing, shop floor, and installation.
- Inventory correctness is still the biggest manufacturing-readiness gate. A clean reconciliation and clear production/dispatch operator proof matrix should be treated as release prerequisites before relying on allocation/backorder/production-plan decisions.
- Delivery/pickup proof needs a server-owned finalization path so drivers can complete work confidently even when mobile connectivity is inconsistent.
- Dealer direct-ship snapshots are documented in the dealership program work. Those snapshots should be visible to fulfillment staff at approval/dispatch time so shipping cannot silently follow edited customer data.
