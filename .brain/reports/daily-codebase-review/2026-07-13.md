## Highest-Risk Findings

- P1 - Operational mutation routes still have inconsistent auth boundaries. Evidence: `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/dispatch.route.ts` uses `publicProcedure` for `deletePackingItem`, `cancelDispatch`, `startDispatch`, `submitDispatch`, driver/status/due-date updates, `sendSaleForPickup`, `signPackingSlip`, duplicate resolution, `deleteDispatch`, `uploadDispatchDocument`, bulk assignment/cancel, export, restore, and more. `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/settings.route.ts` protects app-download settings but leaves generic `updateSetting` public. `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/notes.route.ts` leaves notification channel update/subscriber mutations public. Inference: even when UI hides controls, API-level accountability is not strong enough for dispatch, settings, and notification operations.
- P1 - Dealer requested quotes can still be edited after request state changes. Evidence: `apps/dealership/src/components/tables/quotes/columns.tsx` disables duplicate `Request order` for pending/closed requests but always exposes the `Edit` link. `apps/api/src/db/queries/dealer-portal-sales-form.ts` updates an existing dealer quote by `id`, `dealerAuthId`, `deletedAt`, and `type: "quote"` without selecting or guarding current request status. Impact: sales reps may approve a quote snapshot that the dealer changes afterward.
- P1 - Inventory remains not release-clean. Evidence: `brain/tasks/in-progress.md` and `brain/features/inventory-backed-sales-fulfillment.md` record the latest evidence as `needs_backfill`, `20449` missing sales, `9` shipment/allocation drift rows, `1` skipped comparison, `hasMore=true`, and repairs stopped by user request. Impact: production, inventory, purchasing, dispatch, and customer promise dates should not be treated as fully inventory-authoritative yet.
- P2 - Mobile dispatch proof is not atomic with completion. Evidence: `apps/expo-app/src/features/dispatch/components/dispatch-detail-screen/index.tsx` uploads attachments and signature first through `documents.uploadBase64` / `documents.uploadText`, then submits `actions.onSubmitDispatch` with pathnames. `apps/expo-app/src/features/dispatch/api/use-dispatch-documents.ts` calls `dispatch.uploadDispatchDocument`, which is a public dispatch route. Impact: weak field network can create orphan proof files or failed completion with no single retryable proof bundle.
- P2 - Broad typecheck is blocked before full validation. Evidence: `bun run typecheck` fails in `@gnd/app-store` with missing `node` type definitions and stops the workspace proof. Impact: code health regressions in later packages may be hidden until the first blocker is fixed.

## Worker Usability Review

Sales reps and office admins need crisp ownership and low ambiguity. Public mutation boundaries make it harder to explain who can do what and why an action succeeded. The UI may look permissioned, but route-level ambiguity creates support and audit problems when a worker makes an accidental change.

Production and warehouse workers need confidence that inventory status means real availability. Current Brain evidence still says the inventory cutover is not clean, so training should continue to frame inventory-backed fulfillment as an active cutover, not a finished source of truth.

Drivers/installers have a better completion form than earlier states because it requires signature and supports photos. The weak spot is reliability: proof capture, upload, and completion are separate client steps rather than one retryable work package.

## Customer And Dealer Experience Review

Dealers see a clear `Request order` state in the quote list, which is good. The trust issue is that requested/approved/rejected quote state is not enforced on edit/save. For dealers and customers, a quote under office review should behave like a submitted request, not a normal draft.

Dealer payment-link and order overview routes are protected and scoped to `dealerAuthId`, which is a positive signal. The next dealer-experience improvement should be making the approval snapshot visible: what changed since request, who approved, delivery cost added, and payment status.

Customer communication work is increasingly tracked through email ledger and WhatsApp/SMS plans. Current review did not find a single customer-facing delivery/pickup timeline that ties production readiness, packing, dispatch, proof, and payment into one simple status story.

## Door-Manufacturing Workflow Gaps

- Quote review still lacks a hard submitted snapshot boundary for door dimensions, hand/swing, profile pricing, HPT packages, moulding rows, and delivery option once a dealer requests an order.
- Inventory cutover blockers mean sales promises can still diverge from actual stock, inbound, allocation, production, and dispatch readiness.
- Dispatch proof is present but not operationally durable enough for route completion, pickup acknowledgement, and customer dispute handling.
- Cross-surface status vocabulary remains hard to train: dispatch, packing, production, inventory fulfillment, payment, dealer request, and customer-facing status each have their own surfaces.
