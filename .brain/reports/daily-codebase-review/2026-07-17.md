## Highest-Risk Findings

### P1 - Live Sales Share Sends Tokenized PDF Link To A Hard-Coded Phone Number

Evidence:
- `apps/www/src/components/sales-menu.tsx:546-561` generates a sales PDF token and calls `share(...)` with `recipient: "+234 8186877306"`.
- `apps/www/src/components/sales-menu.tsx:592-607` exposes this as `SalesMenu.Share`.
- `apps/www/src/components/sales-overview-system/sections/quick-actions-bar.tsx:117`, `apps/www/src/components/sheets/sales-overview-sheet/general-action-bar.tsx:131`, and `apps/www/src/components/sheets/sales-overview-sheet/general-footer.tsx:68` mount that action in live order/sheet menus.

Impact:
If a rep clicks Share, a tokenized customer/order document can go to the wrong recipient. This directly affects customer trust, dealer/customer confidentiality, and payment/document handling.

Tracking label: Plan exists. The proper channelized version is scoped in `.brain/plans/2026-07-02-feature-sales-document-whatsapp-sms-delivery.md`, but this hard-coded live action should be removed or disabled before that full feature lands.

### P1 - Dealer Approval Workflow Still Has A Direct Convert Mutation

Evidence:
- `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/dealer-portal.route.ts:385-389` still exposes `dealerPortal.convertQuoteToOrder`.
- `packages/db/src/queries/dealers.ts:3119-3130` converts a dealer quote directly to an order.

## Worker Usability Review

Sales reps need fewer document-send choices that can do the wrong thing. The current `Share` action is too dangerous because it looks like a normal customer communication action but ignores the actual customer phone. The planned WhatsApp/SMS delivery work has the right shape: channel-specific buttons, missing-phone disabled states, short links, and audit results.

Production/inventory workers still need the inventory cutover gates to stay explicit. Brain evidence is clear that the system is not release-clean; training materials should avoid presenting inventory-backed workflows as final until reconciliation, allocation/shipment blockers, and browser proof are complete.

Office/admin staff would benefit from removing raw prompts from dealer approval. `DealerRequestReviewBanner` and `SalesRepDealerRequests` use `window.prompt` for delivery-cost review. It works, but it is easy to mistype and hard to explain to office users compared with a small reviewed-cost form with dealer/customer/order context.

Mobile sales form recovery has meaningful local snapshot support in `apps/expo-app/src/features/sales/invoice-form/lib/local-recovery.ts` and restore handling in `invoice-form-screen.tsx`. That is good for field reliability, but mobile sales parity remains in progress and should continue to prioritize simple save/retry states, offline-ish recovery, and no hidden data-loss paths.

## Customer And Dealer Experience Review

Customers are most exposed through document/payment links. The hard-coded share recipient is the highest customer-trust issue found today because it can send real customer sales documents to the wrong phone.

Dealers now see request-oriented UI in the portal, which is better than direct conversion. The remaining direct convert mutation is a backend contract risk, not a visible UI issue. Removing or server-disabling it would align the contract with the intended dealer experience.

Dealer approval still needs a more guided review surface. The Brain feature calls for quote summary, dealer, customer, selected delivery type, line items, request age, and already-worked state. Current approval prompts cover the required cost field but are not yet a trainable office workflow.

## Door-Manufacturing Workflow Gaps

- Quote/order document sending should be customer-specific and channel-aware. Door quotes often include dimensions, profile choices, line-item prices, and payment links; wrong-recipient sending is not acceptable.
- Dealer quote approval must be the only route to an order. Office review is where delivery cost, dimensions, product profile, swing/hinge choices, and customer/dealer invoice mode are checked.
- Inventory cutover still needs clean reconciliation before production/warehouse teams can rely on inventory-backed readiness, allocation, inbound, and dispatch states as operational truth.
- Delivery/pickup/packing status continues to be a high-value area, but should build on the existing pickup packing funnel and inventory correctness work rather than adding new status language.
