## Highest-Risk Findings

### P0 - Operational upload and mutation routes are exposed as `publicProcedure`

Evidence:

- `apps/api/src/trpc/init.ts:77` defines `publicProcedure` as the primary DB middleware only; `protectedProcedure` adds the `userId` requirement at `apps/api/src/trpc/init.ts:79`.
- `apps/api/src/trpc/middleware/auth-permission.ts` currently passes context through and does not enforce authentication.
- `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/dispatch.route.ts:424` exposes `uploadDispatchDocument` as `publicProcedure`; it accepts caller-supplied `folder`, base64/text content, and uploads through Vercel Blob.
- `apps/api/src/trpc/routers/user.route.ts:74` exposes `uploadDocumentAsset` as `publicProcedure`; it uploads arbitrary base64 content to `employee-documents` before any authenticated save call.
- The targeted scan also showed many dispatch, sales, community, settings, and user mutations using `publicProcedure`.

Impact:

Unauthenticated callers can potentially write files to blob storage and may be able to exercise operational mutations depending on route-level internal checks. For GND, that threatens dispatch proof integrity, employee document accountability, storage cost control, and basic auditability.

Inference:

Some downstream query functions still check `ctx.userId`, so not every public route is automatically unauthenticated-write vulnerable. The upload endpoints above are directly vulnerable by inspection because the upload happens inside the public mutation without an auth check.

### P1 - Dealer quote requests are still mutable after submission

Evidence:

- `apps/dealership/src/components/tables/quotes/columns.tsx:102` derives pending/closed request state and disables only the `Request order` button at `apps/dealership/src/components/tables/quotes/columns.tsx:149`.
- The same row action always keeps `Edit` available at `apps/dealership/src/components/tables/quotes/columns.tsx:115`.
- `apps/api/src/db/queries/dealer-portal-sales-form.ts:355` loads an existing dealer quote by id/dealer/type only, with no `DealerSalesRequest` state check.
- The save path updates the quote at `apps/api/src/db/queries/dealer-portal-sales-form.ts:417`, deletes/recreates all line items at `apps/api/src/db/queries/dealer-portal-sales-form.ts:438`, and rewrites dealer totals at `apps/api/src/db/queries/dealer-portal-sales-form.ts:446`.

Impact:

A dealer can change line items, prices, fulfillment metadata, or totals after requesting order conversion. A sales rep may approve a moving target, which undermines delivery-cost review, customer expectations, invoice trust, and payment-link handoff.

## Worker Usability Review

- Mobile dispatch completion has useful proof primitives: recipient, note type, note, required signature, and optional photos in `apps/expo-app/src/features/dispatch/components/dispatch-complete-form.tsx`.
- The current proof capture is gallery-first, not camera-first. For drivers/installers/warehouse staff, taking a fresh proof photo at the point of work is more trainable than searching the library.
- There is no obvious offline queue/retry state around dispatch proof uploads before submit. A weak network during delivery can leave workers unsure whether proof was saved.
- Inventory screens have a lot of capability gating and read-only language, but the Brain cutover plan still requires browser/operator proof before broad trust.

## Customer And Dealer Experience Review

- Dealer request status is visible enough to prevent duplicate request clicks, but not strong enough to freeze the submitted quote.
- The active WhatsApp/SMS plan fits customer communication needs: short links for quote acceptance, PDF, and payment are practical for customers who do not live in email.
- Dealership quote/order approval needs a clear revision story: locked submitted quote, withdraw/resubmit, or revision requested by sales rep. Without that, dealer users can accidentally invalidate the approval trail.

## Door-Manufacturing Workflow Gaps

- Submitted dealer quotes need immutable production-relevant details: door dimensions, hinge/swing, profile, moulding/service rows, delivery mode, and pricing snapshots should not drift after request without a new revision.
- Inventory cutover evidence still has missing-sales and shipment/allocation blockers, so production readiness, stock allocation, inbound receiving, and dispatch claims should remain guarded.
- Mobile proof-of-delivery should be camera-first and preferably barcode/QR assisted so drivers and warehouse staff can confirm the correct order/dispatch before completing.
- Shared document validation blocks confidence in receipts, dispatch signatures, employee insurance docs, and sales PDFs.
